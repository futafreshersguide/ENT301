Chapter Summary: The Nigerian Entrepreneurial Environment

1. Concept of Business Environment

The business environment refers to the total surroundings—both within and outside an organization—in which a business operates. This environment is never static and is often unpredictable. Understanding it is a primary means of survival for any entrepreneurial venture because the environment creates the opportunities that allow a business to grow and the threats that could cause it to fail.

2. Overview of the Nigerian Business Environment

In the current Nigerian setting, the performance of Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) is significantly affected by a complex and turbulent landscape. Key factors currently impacting Nigerian businesses include:

  • Economic Constraints: High production costs, low sales, and low capital utilization.
  • Resource Gaps: A lack of foreign exchange to source necessary inputs.
  • Infrastructure Challenges: Poor power supply remains a major hurdle.
  • Quality Issues: Difficulty in maintaining high quality for goods and services.

3. Types of Business Environment

The business environment is categorized by the degree of influence an organization has over various forces:

  • Internal Environment (Micro): This includes resources, processes, and policies within the firm. The organization has direct influence over these elements, such as its employees and internal objectives.
  • Performance Environment: This involves stakeholders like competitors, suppliers, and indirect service providers. The organization has a stronger level of influence here than in the macro-environment.
  • External Environment (Macro): These are broad forces—political, economic, social, technological, environmental, and legal—over which a business has relatively little or no influence.

4. Environmental Analysis & Scanning

Environmental Scanning is the process of gathering information about external events and relationships to help top management make decisions. This is done by reviewing company reports, newspapers, and market intelligence. Two primary frameworks are used for analysis:

  • PESTEL Framework: Used to analyze the macro-environment by looking at Political, Economic, Socio-cultural, Technological, Environmental, and Legal factors.
  • SWOT Analysis: Evaluates internal Strengths and Weaknesses, along with external Opportunities and Threats.

5. Performance Effectiveness (SMART)

For an organization to improve performance, its goals and appraisal systems must meet the SMART criteria:

  • Specific
  • Measurable
  • Attainable
  • Reliable
  • Time-bound

Points to Note & Exam Hints

  • The Role of SMEs: Small and Medium Enterprises are vital for economic development, contributing significantly to the Gross Domestic Product (GDP), income generation, and poverty reduction.
  • Market Business Environment: Major players include the banking sector, insurance, stock exchanges, and pension administrators. Their primary goals are profit maximization and cost minimization.
  • Regulation: In Nigeria, bodies like the Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) and the Nigeria Deposit Insurance Commission (NDIC) regulate and monitor the operations of commercial banks.
  • Lobbying & CSR: Businesses sometimes lobby the government for favorable policies and, in return, must prioritize Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) to the communities where they operate.

Mnemonics to Note

1. Macro-Environmental Analysis: "P.E.S.T.E.L."

  • Political (Power changes, tax regimes, industrial actions)
  • Economic (Inflation, interest rates, exchange rates, GDP)
  • Socio-cultural (Consumer tastes, religion, demographics, lifestyle)
  • Technological (Efficiency, communication, global competition)
  • Environmental (External conditions affecting objectives)
  • Legal (Pricing transparency, minimum wage, safety regulations)

2. Strategic Planning Tool: "S.W.O.T."

  • Strengths (Internal: Brand strength, technology prowess, efficiency)
  • Weaknesses (Internal: Lack of expertise, poor infrastructure)
  • Opportunities (External: Strategic alliances, policy changes)
  • Threats (External: Currency fluctuations, changing customer tastes)

3. Goal Setting: "S.M.A.R.T."

  • Specific
  • Measurable
  • Attainable
  • Reliable
  • Time-bound
